About

I’m Luis Gonzalez, an Electrical and Computer Engineering undergraduate at UTSA’s Klesse College of Engineering and an Honors Roadrunner. I’m passionate about hands-on engineering, where I combine technical skills with creativity to build practical, real-world solutions. I’ve led and contributed to projects across robotics, assistive technology, and embedded systems, including a humanoid robot, Bluetooth-controlled devices, and an eye-tracking interface for ALS patients. As Vice President of EPICS and an executive officer in UTSA’s IEEE Robotics and Automation Society, I’m actively involved in technical leadership, collaboration, and mentorship. With strong experience in C/C++, microcontrollers, PCB design, and computer vision, I’m always looking for opportunities to innovate and make a lasting impact through engineering.
